♥ Daily Helping Hygenic Tip ♥

#345: Too many hairballs? Overgrooming is common in many cats and stems from boredom or loneliness. To combat this consider playing with your cat more to purchasing more entertainment for them to keep themselves busy with rather than self grooming. Another method of handling this can be to trim your cat's coat or even visit a vetinary professional and ask them on what they suggest for your specific cat, as each breed is different. If your cat is prone to hairballs a quick mixture of baking soda and lemon juice with a teaspoon of dishsoap should scrub away any debris they leave behind.

Which Essential Oils are safe?

Many essential oils such as: Lemon, Eucalyptus, Sawgrass, Lavender, Tea Tree, and Peppermint are harmful and even lethal to your cat. Make sure to avoid these at all costs!

Outdoor vs. Indoor

Cat's are either indoor cats, outdoor cats, or a little bit of both. We're here to report that it is much safer for your cat to remain indoor as much as possible. Cat's who remain indoors have a 60% chance of staying disease free to infections and a 30% chance of a longer and healthier life. Overall keeping your cat indoors is more beneficial to their health in the long run.

The Debate on Declawment

Declawing your cat should be a complete last resort. Declawing a cat's paw is the human equivalent to cutting off your fist digit of your fingers. It can be seen as cruel and may even result in joint issues later on in your furry friends life. The best way to deal with claws is to trim them monthly either professionally or on your own. Another method is to purchase nail caps for your cat either full time or in between trimmings.
